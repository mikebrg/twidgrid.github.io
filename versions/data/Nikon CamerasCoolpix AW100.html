Nikon Cameras Coolpix AW100

The Nikon Coolpix AW100 is a rugged, weatherproof compact digital camera released in 2011, designed for outdoor activities and underwater use. It is waterproof, shockproof, and freeze-proof, and features a 16-megapixel CMOS sensor, 5x optical zoom lens, and built-in GPS.

Reviews and Performance

Image Quality: The camera produces good images in bright, outdoor conditions, though some reviewers noted softness in the corners of photos. Image noise becomes visible at higher ISO settings (ISO 800 and above), and the camera is not a strong low-light performer due to its relatively slow lens aperture (f/3.9).

Video Quality: Video performance is generally considered good, offering full 1080p HD recording at 30fps with the ability to use the optical zoom during recording. The camera also offers slow-motion and fast-motion movie options.

Durability and Handling: The rugged build is a major selling point, and the camera has been praised for reliably surviving drops and water exposure. The "Action Control" feature, which allows users to change some settings by shaking the camera, is helpful when wearing gloves. However, some reviewers found the small, spongy control buttons difficult to operate, especially when wet or at depth underwater.

GPS and Features: The built-in GPS and e-Compass functions are useful for geotagging photos and tracking routes, which can be viewed on an in-camera map display. Some users found the GPS functions difficult to set up initially without consulting the manual.
