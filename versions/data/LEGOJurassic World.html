LEGO Jurassic World

The LEGO Jurassic World theme is a popular, ongoing licensed product line launched in June 2015, featuring construction sets and a video game based on the entire Jurassic Park and Jurassic World film franchises, as well as related animated series. The sets are known for their detailed dinosaur figures and minifigures of key characters.

Overview

Initial Release: The theme was introduced in 2015 to coincide with the release of the Jurassic World film.

Franchise Coverage: It has since expanded to include sets based on the original Jurassic Park movie, The Lost World: Jurassic Park, Jurassic Park III, Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom, Jurassic World Dominion, the Camp Cretaceous animated series, and the upcoming Jurassic World Rebirth.

Product Types: The line includes standard construction sets for various age groups, larger 18+ collector sets, LEGO Duplo sets for toddlers, and a popular video game that adapts the storylines of the first four films.

Collectibility: Key features of the sets are the large, molded dinosaur figures (like the T. rex, Velociraptor, and Indominus rex) and detailed minifigures of characters such as Owen Grady, Claire Dearing, Dr. Alan Grant, and Dr. Ellie Sattler.

Key Set Examples

The theme offers a diverse range of sets, from action-packed vehicle chases to detailed display models.

The Mystery Machine (75902): Although this is from the separate LEGO Scooby-Doo theme, it's a popular vehicle set from a related time period.

Jurassic Park Velociraptor Chase (75932): A set from 2018 that recreates the iconic computer control room and kitchen scene from the original 1993 film, including minifigures of Alan Grant and Ellie Sattler.

Jurassic Park: T. rex Rampage (75936): A large, detailed set released in 2019 featuring the iconic main gates of the park and a massive, posable T. rex figure, primarily a display piece aimed at adult collectors.

Indominus rex Breakout (75919 / 75941): Sets released in 2015 and 2020 featuring the genetically engineered Indominus rex dinosaur and parts of its enclosure.

Giganotosaurus & Therizinosaurus Attack (76949): A large set from the Jurassic World Dominion wave, including two large dinosaur figures and a detailed lab section.

Video Game

The LEGO Jurassic World video game was released in June 2015 for numerous platforms, including PC, PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch. The game allows players to: Relive key moments and explore the islands of Isla Nublar and Isla Sorna from all four films, rendered with classic LEGO humor.

Play as more than 100 unlockable characters, including over 20 different dinosaur species.

Customize dinosaurs by collecting LEGO amber and experimenting with DNA to create new species, such as the "Dilophosaurus Rex".
