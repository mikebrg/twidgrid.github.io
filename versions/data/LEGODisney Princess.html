LEGO Disney Princess

The LEGO Disney Princess theme, originally launched in 2014, features construction sets based on beloved Disney films, with a strong emphasis on castles, vehicles, and the iconic princess characters. The theme continues to be an active part of the broader LEGO Disney product line.

Overview

Figures: The sets primarily use a unique "mini-doll" figure type, which is more detailed and realistic than the traditional LEGO minifigure, similar to those found in the LEGO Friends theme.

Focus: The sets are typically geared toward younger builders (ages 4 and up) with simpler designs and Starter Bricks, but the line also includes larger, more complex adult collector sets.

Media: The theme is supported by animated web shorts and streaming specials on Disney+, such as LEGO Disney Princess: The Castle Quest (2023) and the 2025 sequel LEGO Disney Princess: Villains Unite.

Featured Characters and Films

The theme covers a wide range of Disney properties and characters:

Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs: Snow White, Evil Queen, the Magic Mirror.

Cinderella: Cinderella, Prince Charming, the Fairy Godmother.

Sleeping Beauty: Aurora (Sleeping Beauty), Maleficent.

The Little Mermaid: Ariel, Prince Eric, King Triton, Ursula, Flounder.

Beauty and the Beast: Belle, the Beast (Prince Adam), Gaston, Lumière, Cogsworth, Mrs. Potts, Chip.

Aladdin: Jasmine, Jafar, Genie, Apu, Rajah.

Mulan: Mulan, Mushu.

The Princess and the Frog: Tiana, Prince Naveen, Dr. Facilier.

Tangled: Rapunzel, Flynn Rider (Eugene Fitzherbert), Pascal.

Brave: Merida.

Frozen: Anna, Elsa, Olaf, Kristoff, Sven.

Moana: Moana, Maui, Pua, Heihei.

Raya and the Last Dragon: Raya, Sisu.

Encanto: Mirabel, Antonio, Abuela Alma.

Notable Sets

The theme includes numerous sets, often recreating iconic scenes and locations from the films:

The Disney Castle (71040/43222): Large, highly detailed display models featuring many Easter eggs and minifigures representing various princesses.

The Simpsons House (71006): Not from the Disney Princess theme, but part of the broader LEGO Disney umbrella.

Elsa's Sparkling Ice Palace (41062/43197): Iconic castle builds from the Frozen franchise.

The Ultimate Adventure Castle (43205): A set from 2022 that brought together five different princesses in one multi-level castle build.

The Kwik-E-Mart (71016): Like The Simpsons House, this is a separate licensed set from the LEGO The Simpsons line.
