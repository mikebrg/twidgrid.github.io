LEGO Ultra Agents

LEGO Ultra Agents was a discontinued action theme that ran from 2014 to 2015, focusing on an elite team of agents using high-tech gear to protect Astor City from a wide range of super villains, and was notable for integrating physical play with a digital app experience.

Theme Overview

The theme was considered an update to the previous LEGO Agents line and featured a strong narrative that played out across 14 unique construction sets and an interactive comic book app.

Storyline: The Ultra Agents, led by veteran Agent Solomon Blaze (a returning character from the LEGO Galaxy Squad theme), battled "disaster-themed" villains like Infearno (fire), Tremor (earthquakes), and Toxikita (toxic spills). The main antagonist was AntiMatter, a corrupted former agent whose staff could turn citizens into evil clones.

App Integration: Each set included "AppBricks"—special LEGO elements that could be scanned using a free iOS and Android app to unlock new missions, clues, and secret build instructions, enhancing the play experience.

Design Aesthetic: The Ultra Agents' vehicles had a consistent futuristic color scheme of black, silver, and transparent blue, while the villains' vehicles matched their individual color and elemental themes.

Key Characters

The theme featured a dedicated cast of agents and villains, each with unique backstories and gadgets.

Ultra Agents

Agent Max Burns: The team's expert driver.

Agent Jack Fury: The daring daredevil of the team.

Agent Caila Phoenix: A demolitions expert and martial artist.

Agent Curtis Bolt: The team's inventor and gear specialist.

Agent Solomon Blaze: The leader of the team and a former Galaxy Squad member.

Villains

AntiMatter: The main antagonist of the series.

Toxikita: A former environmentalist turned villainess obsessed with toxic waste.

Infearno: A former firefighter with a flaming hoverboard.

Tremor: A heavily armored villain who was once a local champion boxer.

Psyclone: A former weathercaster who controls the weather.

Collectibility

The LEGO Ultra Agents theme was discontinued by the end of 2015. Due to their retirement and unique blend of physical and digital play, the sets and minifigures are popular among collectors and can be found on secondary markets.
