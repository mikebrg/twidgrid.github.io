LEGO Spider-Man

The LEGO Spider-Man theme is an ongoing product line first introduced in 2002, now part of the larger LEGO Marvel Super Heroes franchise. It features a vast collection of sets, minifigures, and vehicles based on the comics, films, and animated series.

History of the Theme

The LEGO Spider-Man theme has evolved over several distinct periods:

Original Run (2002-2004): The initial sets were part of the LEGO Studios line and were based on the Spider-Man and Spider-Man 2 films.

Relaunch as Marvel Super Heroes (2012 onwards): After a hiatus, the theme returned as a sub-theme of the broader LEGO Marvel Super Heroes line, incorporating characters from various comics and the Ultimate Spider-Man animated series.

Movie Tie-ins: Since the return, LEGO has released numerous sets tied to the Marvel Cinematic Universe films (Homecoming, Far From Home, No Way Home) as well as large collector's sets based on the comics and other media.

Current and Available Sets

LEGO continues to release new Spider-Man sets catering to different age groups and interests.

Spider-Man Final Battle (76261): This set recreates the iconic showdown from Spider-Man: No Way Home, including minifigures of all three film versions of Spider-Man (Tobey Maguire, Andrew Garfield, and Tom Holland) as well as Doctor Strange, Green Goblin, Doc Ock, and others.

Daily Bugle (76178): A massive, detailed modular building set for adults, standing over 32 inches (82 cm) tall. It includes 25 minifigures, many of which are exclusive to this set, such as Daredevil, Punisher, and Blade.

Spider-Man vs. Doc Ock Subway Train Scene (76321): A play set featuring a detailed subway car model and four minifigures, including Spider-Man and Doc Ock.

Buildable Figures: LEGO offers large, fully articulated figures for play and display, such as the LEGO Marvel Spider-Man Figure (76226) and the Iron Spider-Man Construction Figure (76303).

4+ Sets: Simpler sets designed for younger builders, such as Spider-Man's Car and Doc Ock (set 10789), using a special Starter Brick chassis to encourage building.

Discontinued and Collectible Sets Many past Spider-Man sets are now retired and can be valuable collector's items.

Spider-Man's Train Rescue (4855): A popular set from the Spider-Man 2 film tie-in, now sought after by collectors.

Spider-Man: Web Warriors Ultimate Bridge Battle (76057): A large bridge set from 2016 which has appreciated in value since retirement.

Daily Bugle Showdown (76005): An earlier, smaller version of the Daily Bugle, which is now a valuable set on the secondary market.
