Nikon Cameras Coolpix AW120

The Nikon Coolpix AW120 is a rugged, weatherproof compact digital camera released in 2014, featuring built-in Wi-Fi and an enhanced depth rating compared to its predecessors. It is waterproof to 59 feet (18m), shockproof from 6.6 feet (2m), and freezeproof to 14°F (-10°C), making it suitable for a variety of outdoor activities.

Reviews and Performance

Image Quality: According to reviewers, the camera produces good images in bright, outdoor conditions, with vibrant colors. However, fine detail can appear soft due to aggressive noise reduction at higher ISO settings (ISO 400 and above). The fast f/2.8 wide aperture of the lens is a plus for low-light shooting within its class.

Video Quality: The AW120 is praised for its ability to record smooth Full HD 1080p video, with the optical zoom and autofocus available during recording. The 5-axis hybrid vibration reduction helps in getting steady footage.

Durability and Handling: The camera's robust build is a key selling point, with the increased 59-foot depth rating making it highly suitable for snorkeling and recreational diving (for those with a basic diving license). The Action Control feature is useful when operating the camera while wearing gloves. However, some reviewers noted that the small rear buttons could be tricky to use, especially when wet, and the lack of a prominent front grip can make one-handed shooting feel less secure.

GPS and Features: The integrated GPS, GLONASS support, e-Compass, and world map display are comprehensive features that allow users to geotag photos, log routes, and view location data on the camera monitor. The built-in Wi-Fi makes it easy to transfer images to a smart device for instant sharing. Battery life is rated at approximately 350 shots per charge, though this is reduced significantly with heavy use of GPS and Wi-Fi features.
