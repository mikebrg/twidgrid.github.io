Old Testament

The Old Testament is a collection of ancient texts that tell the story of God’s creation, his covenant with Israel, and their journey of faith, struggle, and hope for a promised future.

  • Genesis

    Describes the creation of the world, the beginnings of humanity, and the stories of the early patriarchs, including Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph.

  • Exodus

    Tells how God rescued the Israelites from Egypt through Moses and established them as His chosen people.

  • Leviticus

    Outlines the laws and rituals for worship, sacrifice, and holy living among God’s people.

  • Numbers

    Chronicles the Israelites' journey through the wilderness, highlighting their struggles, disobedience, and God’s guidance.

  • Deuteronomy

    Moses' farewell speeches, restating God’s laws as the Israelites prepare to enter the Promised Land.

  • Joshua

    Details the conquest and settlement of the Promised Land under Joshua’s leadership.

  • Judges

    Recounts the cycles of Israel’s disobedience, oppression, and deliverance by judges appointed by God.

  • Ruth

    A story of loyalty and redemption, following Ruth and her faithfulness, leading to her place in Israel’s lineage.

  • 1 Samuel

    Describes the life of Samuel, Israel’s transition to monarchy, and the rise of King Saul.

  • 2 Samuel

    Chronicles King David’s reign, his triumphs, and his personal and political challenges.

  • 1 Kings

    Covers the reign of Solomon, the division of Israel into two kingdoms, and the early kings of both.

  • 2 Kings

    Details the decline and fall of both Israel and Judah due to their disobedience to God.

  • 1 Chronicles

    Retells the history of David’s rule and emphasizes God’s covenant with Israel.

  • 2 Chronicles

    Chronicles the kings of Judah, focusing on temple worship and faithfulness to God.

  • Ezra

    Describes the return of exiles to Jerusalem and the rebuilding of the temple.

  • Nehemiah

    Tells of the rebuilding of Jerusalem’s walls and the restoration of the community.

  • Tobit

    A story of faithfulness and divine providence, following Tobit, a righteous Israelite, and his son Tobias, who is guided by the angel Raphael to find healing and deliverance for their family.

  • Judith

    Tells the story of Judith, a courageous Jewish widow, who saves her people by using her wit and bravery to defeat an enemy general threatening Israel.

  • Esther

    The story of Queen Esther, who risks her life to save her people from destruction.

  • 1 Maccabees

    Chronicles the struggle of the Jewish people to gain independence from Greek rule, highlighting the heroic efforts of the Maccabee family to restore religious freedom and cleanse the Temple.

  • 2 Maccabees

    Focuses on the religious and moral aspects of the Jewish struggle against Greek oppression, emphasizing God’s intervention, martyrdom, and the hope of resurrection for those who remain faithful.

  • Job

    Explores the themes of suffering, faith, and God’s sovereignty through the trials of Job.

  • Psalms

    A collection of songs and prayers expressing worship, lament, praise, and thanksgiving.

  • Proverbs

    A compilation of wise sayings providing practical guidance for righteous living.

  • Ecclesiastes

    Reflects on the meaning of life, acknowledging life’s vanity without God.

  • The Song of Songs

    A poetic celebration of love between a bride and groom, often seen as symbolic of God’s love.

  • The Book of Wisdom

    Explores the nature of wisdom, urging readers to seek righteousness, resist idolatry, and trust in God’s guidance and justice, with reflections on the rewards of a wise and faithful life.

  • Sirach

    Offers practical wisdom on living a virtuous life, covering topics like friendship, humility, family, and honoring God, while emphasizing the importance of wisdom rooted in reverence for the Lord.

  • Isaiah

    Contains prophecies of warning and comfort, pointing to a future Messiah.

  • Jeremiah

    Proclaims God’s judgment against Judah and promises restoration.

  • Lamentations

    Poems lamenting the fall of Jerusalem and expressing hope in God’s mercy.

  • Baruch

    A collection of prayers and reflections attributed to Baruch, the scribe of the prophet Jeremiah, calling the Israelites to repentance, acknowledging their sins, and seeking God's mercy and restoration.

  • Ezekiel

    Contains visions of judgment and hope for Israel’s restoration.

  • Daniel

    Tells stories of faithfulness under persecution and visions of future kingdoms.

  • Hosea

    Uses the prophet’s marriage to symbolize God’s love for unfaithful Israel.

  • Joel

    Warns of a coming locust plague and calls for repentance, promising future blessing.

  • Amos

    Calls for social justice and condemns Israel’s hypocrisy and corruption.

  • Obadiah

    Foretells the downfall of Edom and the restoration of Israel.

  • Jonah

    A prophet runs from God, is swallowed by a fish, and ultimately preaches repentance in Nineveh.

  • Micah

    Warns of judgment and offers hope for a future ruler from Bethlehem.

  • Nahum

    Prophesies the destruction of Nineveh as God’s judgment.

  • Habakkuk

    Questions God’s use of evil nations and affirms trust in God’s justice.

  • Zephaniah

    Foretells judgment on Jerusalem and the nations but promises eventual restoration.

  • Haggai

    Urges the people to rebuild the temple after the exile.

  • Zechariah

    Offers visions of hope and encouragement for the returning exiles.

  • Malachi

    Calls out the people’s spiritual apathy and foretells the coming of a new messenger.
