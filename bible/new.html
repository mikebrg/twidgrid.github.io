New Testament

The New Testament is a collection of books that tell the story of Jesus Christ's life, teachings, death, and resurrection, along with letters guiding early Christians on how to live according to his teachings.

Home Search Pentateuch Historical Books Wisdom Books Prophetic Books Gospels New Testament Letters Catholic Letters

  • Matthew

    A Gospel presenting Jesus as the promised Messiah and King of Israel.

  • Mark

    A fast-paced Gospel highlighting Jesus’ authority and servanthood.

  • Luke

    A detailed Gospel emphasizing Jesus’ compassion for all, including Gentiles and the marginalized.

  • John

    A Gospel focusing on Jesus’ identity as the Son of God and the giver of eternal life.

  • Acts

    Chronicles the spread of the Gospel and the early church through the apostles.

  • Romans

    Paul’s letter explaining salvation by faith and the transformation it brings.

  • 1 Corinthians

    Addresses issues within the Corinthian church and calls for unity and love.

  • 2 Corinthians

    Paul defends his apostleship and encourages generosity and reconciliation.

  • Galatians

    Emphasizes salvation by grace through faith, not by following the law.

  • Ephesians

    Describes the unity of believers in Christ and offers practical advice for Christian living.

  • Philippians

    A letter of joy, encouraging perseverance and humility in Christ.

  • Colossians

    Warns against false teachings and celebrates Christ’s supremacy.

  • 1 Thessalonians

    Encourages believers to live in holiness and hope for Jesus’ return.

  • 2 Thessalonians

    Addresses misunderstandings about Christ’s return and calls for steadfastness.

  • 1 Timothy

    Provides guidance for church leadership and godly living.

  • 2 Timothy

    Paul’s final letter urging Timothy to remain faithful and courageous.

  • Titus

    Offers instructions for church order and sound doctrine.

  • Philemon

    A personal letter urging forgiveness and reconciliation between a master and his slave.

  • Hebrews

    Emphasizes Christ’s superiority over the old covenant and encourages faith.

  • James

    Teaches that genuine faith is shown through good works.

  • 1 Peter

    Offers hope and guidance to Christians facing persecution.

  • 2 Peter

    Warns against false teachers and reminds believers of Christ’s return.

  • 1 John

    Stresses love and fellowship with God and others as marks of true faith.

  • 2 John

    Encourages truth and love while warning against false teachings.

  • 3 John

    Praises hospitality and faithfulness within the Christian community.

  • Jude

    Warns against false teachers and urges believers to stay faithful.

  • Revelation

    A vision of the end times, promising victory for Christ and a new creation for believers.
