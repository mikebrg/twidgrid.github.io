August

August is the eighth month of the year in the Julian and Gregorian calendars. Its length is 31 days.

August 1

Christopher Columbus becomes the first European to visit what is now Venezuela.
Venezuela

1498 AD

British scientist Joseph Priestley discovers oxygen gas, corroborating the prior discovery of this element by German-Swedish chemist Carl Wilhelm Scheele.
England

1774 AD

The start of the first Scout camp on Brownsea Island, the origin of the worldwide Scouting movement.
Brownsea Island,  Poole Harbour, England

1907 AD

August 2

A Macedonian army led by Philip II defeated the combined forces of Athens and Thebes in the Battle of Chaeronea, securing Macedonian hegemony in Greece and the Aegean.
Chaeronea, Boeotia, Greece

338 BC

Tower Subway, the world's first underground tube railway, opens in London, England, United Kingdom.
London, England

1870 AD

Albert Einstein and Leo Szilard write a letter to Franklin D. Roosevelt, urging him to begin the Manhattan Project to develop a nuclear weapon.
Long Island, New York, United States

1939 AD

August 3

The first known letter from North America is sent by John Rut while at St. John's, Newfoundland.
St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada

1527 AD

The Firestone Tire and Rubber Company is founded.
Akron, Ohio, United States

1900 AD

Tandy Corporation announces the TRS-80, one of the world's first mass-produced personal computers.
United States

1977 AD

August 4

Goguryeo-Sui War: In response to a Goguryeo (Korean) incursion into Liaoxi, Emperor Wéndi of Sui orders his youngest son, Yang Liang (assisted by the co-prime minister Gao Jiong), to conquer Goguryeo during the Manchurian rainy season, with a Chinese army and navy.
Manchuria, Northern Korean Peninsula, Yellow Sea

598 AD

Date traditionally ascribed to Dom Perignon's invention of champagne; it is not clear whether he actually invented champagne, however he has been credited as an innovator who developed the techniques used to perfect sparkling wine.
France

1693 AD

NASA's Phoenix spacecraft is launched.
Cape Canaveral SLC-17, Florida, United States

2007 AD

August 5

Sir Humphrey Gilbert establishes the first English colony in North America, at what is now St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador.
St. John's, Newfoundland

1583 AD

The cornerstone for the Statue of Liberty is laid on Bedloe's Island (now Liberty Island) in New York Harbor.
New York, United States

1884 AD

American actress Marilyn Monroe is found dead at her home from a drug overdose.
12305 Fifth Helena Drive, Brentwood, Los Angeles, California, United States

1962 AD

August 6

The Republic of Pisa is defeated in the Battle of Meloria by the Republic of Genoa, thus losing its naval dominance in the Mediterranean.
Islet of Meloria, Ligurian Sea, Italy

1284 AD

At Auburn Prison in New York, murderer William Kemmler becomes the first person to be executed by electric chair.
New York, United States

1890 AD

World War II: Hiroshima, Japan is devastated when the atomic bomb "Little Boy" is dropped by the United States B-29 Enola Gay. Around 70,000 people are killed instantly, and some tens of thousands die in subsequent years from burns and radiation poisoning.
Hiroshima, Japan

1945 AD

August 7

Roman Emperor Majorian is beheaded near the river Iria in north-west Italy following his arrest and deposition by the magister militum Ricimer.
Dertona, Roman Empire

461 AD

George Washington orders the creation of the Badge of Military Merit to honor soldiers wounded in battle. It is later renamed to the more poetic Purple Heart.
United States

1782 AD

IBM dedicates the first program-controlled calculator, the Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator (known best as the Harvard Mark I).
Harvard University,  Cambridge, Massachusetts, United States

1944 AD
