June

Named after Juno, the Roman goddess of marriage and childbirth. This is why June is traditionally considered a popular month for weddings in many cultures!

Calendar

June 1

  • A monk, John Cor, records the first known batch of Scotch whisky.

    1495 AD

    Scotland

  • The United States Census Bureau begins using Herman Hollerith's tabulating machine to count census returns.

    1890 AD

    United States

  • Cable News Network (CNN) begins broadcasting.

    1980 AD

    United States

June 2

  • Sack of Rome: Vandals enter Rome, and plunder the city for two weeks.

    455 AD

    Rome, Italy, Western Roman Empire (present-day Rome, Italy)

  • Guglielmo Marconi applies for a patent for his wireless telegraph.

    1896 AD

    London, England

  • Europe launches its first voyage to another planet, Mars. The European Space Agency's Mars Express probe launches from the Baikonur space center in Kazakhstan.

    2003 AD

    Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan

June 3

  • The Roman usurper Nepotianus, of the Constantinian dynasty, proclaims himself Roman emperor, entering Rome at the head of a group of gladiators.

    350 AD

    Roman Empire

  • The first long-distance electric power transmission line in the United States is completed, running 14 miles (23 km) between a generator at Willamette Falls and downtown Portland, Oregon.

    1889 AD

    Oregon, United States

  • The launch of Gemini 4, the first multi-day space mission by a NASA crew. Ed White, a crew member, performs the first American spacewalk.

    1965 AD

    Cape Kennedy LC-19, Florida, United States

June 4

  • The steeple of St Paul's, the medieval cathedral of London, is destroyed in a fire caused by lightning and is never rebuilt.

    1561 AD

    London, England

  • The Montgolfier brothers publicly demonstrate their montgolfière (hot air balloon).

    1783 AD

    Annonay, France

  • JVC introduces its VHS videotape at the Consumer Electronics Show in Chicago. It will eventually prevail against Sony's rival Betamax system in a format war to become the predominant home video medium.

    1977 AD

    Chicago, United States

June 5

  • Tutush, brother of Seljuk sultan Malik Shah, defeats Suleiman ibn Qutalmish, the Turkish ruler of Anatolia in the battle of Ain Salm.

    1086 AD

    Aleppo, Syria

  • The first Great Lakes steamer, the Frontenac, is launched.

    1817 AD

    Great Lakes, Canada

  • Elvis Presley introduces his new single, "Hound Dog", on The Milton Berle Show, scandalizing the audience with his suggestive hip movements.

    1956 AD

    United States

June 6

  • Constantine VII, the eight-year-old illegitimate son of Leo VI the Wise, becomes nominal ruler of the Byzantine Empire under the regency of a seven-man council headed by Patriarch Nicholas Mystikos, appointed by Constantine's uncle Alexander on his deathbed.

    913 AD

    Byzantine Empire

  • The Chicago "L" elevated rail system begins operation.

    1892 AD

    Chicago, Illinois, United States

  • The first drive-in theater opens in Camden, New Jersey.

    1933 AD

    Camden, New Jersey, United States

June 7

  • Spain and Portugal sign the Treaty of Tordesillas which divides the New World between the two countries.

    1494 AD

    Tordesillas, Spain

  • Homer Plessy is arrested for refusing to leave his seat in the "whites-only" car of a train; he lost the resulting court case, Plessy v. Ferguson.

    1892 AD

    New Orleans, Louisiana, United States

  • The Lateran Treaty is ratified, bringing Vatican City into existence.

    1929 AD

    Rome, Italy
