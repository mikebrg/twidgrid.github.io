June

Named after Juno, the Roman goddess of marriage and childbirth. This is why June is traditionally considered a popular month for weddings in many cultures!

Calendar

June 1

  • A monk, John Cor, records the first known batch of Scotch whisky.

    1495 AD

    Scotland

  • The United States Census Bureau begins using Herman Hollerith's tabulating machine to count census returns.

    1890 AD

    United States

  • Cable News Network (CNN) begins broadcasting.

    1980 AD

    United States

June 2

  • Sack of Rome: Vandals enter Rome, and plunder the city for two weeks.

    455 AD

    Rome, Italy, Western Roman Empire (present-day Rome, Italy)

  • Guglielmo Marconi applies for a patent for his wireless telegraph.

    1896 AD

    London, England

  • Europe launches its first voyage to another planet, Mars. The European Space Agency's Mars Express probe launches from the Baikonur space center in Kazakhstan.

    2003 AD

    Baikonur Cosmodrome, Kazakhstan

June 3

  • The Roman usurper Nepotianus, of the Constantinian dynasty, proclaims himself Roman emperor, entering Rome at the head of a group of gladiators.

    350 AD

    Roman Empire

  • The first long-distance electric power transmission line in the United States is completed, running 14 miles (23 km) between a generator at Willamette Falls and downtown Portland, Oregon.

    1889 AD

    Oregon, United States

  • The launch of Gemini 4, the first multi-day space mission by a NASA crew. Ed White, a crew member, performs the first American spacewalk.

    1965 AD

    Cape Kennedy LC-19, Florida, United States

June 4

  • The steeple of St Paul's, the medieval cathedral of London, is destroyed in a fire caused by lightning and is never rebuilt.

    1561 AD

    London, England

  • The Montgolfier brothers publicly demonstrate their montgolfière (hot air balloon).

    1783 AD

    Annonay, France

  • JVC introduces its VHS videotape at the Consumer Electronics Show in Chicago. It will eventually prevail against Sony's rival Betamax system in a format war to become the predominant home video medium.

    1977 AD

    Chicago, United States

June 5

  • Tutush, brother of Seljuk sultan Malik Shah, defeats Suleiman ibn Qutalmish, the Turkish ruler of Anatolia in the battle of Ain Salm.

    1086 AD

    Aleppo, Syria

  • The first Great Lakes steamer, the Frontenac, is launched.

    1817 AD

    Great Lakes, Canada

  • Elvis Presley introduces his new single, "Hound Dog", on The Milton Berle Show, scandalizing the audience with his suggestive hip movements.

    1956 AD

    United States

June 6

  • Constantine VII, the eight-year-old illegitimate son of Leo VI the Wise, becomes nominal ruler of the Byzantine Empire under the regency of a seven-man council headed by Patriarch Nicholas Mystikos, appointed by Constantine's uncle Alexander on his deathbed.

    913 AD

    Byzantine Empire

  • The Chicago "L" elevated rail system begins operation.

    1892 AD

    Chicago, Illinois, United States

  • The first drive-in theater opens in Camden, New Jersey.

    1933 AD

    Camden, New Jersey, United States

June 7

  • Spain and Portugal sign the Treaty of Tordesillas which divides the New World between the two countries.

    1494 AD

    Tordesillas, Spain

  • Homer Plessy is arrested for refusing to leave his seat in the "whites-only" car of a train; he lost the resulting court case, Plessy v. Ferguson.

    1892 AD

    New Orleans, Louisiana, United States

  • The Lateran Treaty is ratified, bringing Vatican City into existence.

    1929 AD

    Rome, Italy

June 8

  • Attila leads a Hun army in the invasion of Italy, devastating the northern provinces as he heads for Rome.

    452 AD

    Italy

  • Herman Hollerith applies for US patent #395,781 for the 'Art of Compiling Statistics', which was his punched card calculator.

    1887 AD

    United States

  • USS Barbero and the United States Postal Service attempt the delivery of mail via Missile Mail.

    1959 AD

    United States

June 9

  • Nero dies by suicide after quoting Vergil's Aeneid, thus ending the Julio-Claudian dynasty and starting the civil war known as the Year of the Four Emperors.

    68 AD

    Rome

  • Treaty of Tientsin is signed to end the Sino-French War, with China eventually giving up Tonkin and Annam – most of present-day Vietnam – to France.

    1885 AD

    China

  • The USS George Washington is launched. It is the first nuclear-powered ballistic missile submarine.

    1959 AD

    United States

June 10

  • Emperor Tenji of Japan introduces a water clock (clepsydra) called Rokoku. The instrument, which measures time and indicates hours, is placed in the capital of Ōtsu.

    671 AD

    Japan

  • The Jardin des Plantes museum opens in Paris. A year later, it becomes the first public zoo.

    1793 AD

    France

  • Saab produces its first automobile.

    1947 AD

    Sweden

June 11

  • Henry VIII of England marries Catherine of Aragon.

    1509 AD

    England

  • Paris–Bordeaux–Paris, sometimes called the first automobile race in history or the "first motor race", takes place.

    1895 AD

    France

  • Inventor Edwin Armstrong demonstrates FM broadcasting to an audience of engineers at the FCC in Washington, DC.

    1936 AD

    United States

June 12

  • The city of Helsinki, Finland (belonging to Sweden at the time) is founded by King Gustav I of Sweden.

    1550 AD

    Finland

  • The earliest form of bicycle, the dandy horse, is driven by Karl von Drais.

    1817 AD

    Germany

  • Anne Frank receives a diary for her thirteenth birthday.

    1942 AD

    Netherlands

June 13

  • Henry Grace à Dieu, at over 1,000 tons the largest warship in the world at this time, built at the new Woolwich Dockyard in England, is dedicated.

    1514 AD

    England

  • Yukon Territory is formed, with Dawson chosen as its capital.

    1898 AD

    Canada

  • Pioneer 10 becomes the first man-made object to leave the central Solar System when it passes beyond the orbit of Neptune.

    1983 AD

    United States

June 14

  • The city of Munich is founded by Henry the Lion on the banks of the river Isar.

    1158 AD

    Germany

  • Hawaii becomes a United States territory.

    1900 AD

    United States

  • Disneyland Monorail System, the first daily operating monorail system in the Western Hemisphere, opens to the public in Anaheim, California.

    1959 AD

    United States

June 15

  • Christopher Columbus lands on the island of Martinique on his fourth voyage.

    1502 AD

    Martinique

  • Eadweard Muybridge takes a series of photographs to prove that all four feet of a horse leave the ground when it runs; the study becomes the basis of motion pictures.

    1878 AD

    United States

  • Nik Wallenda becomes the first person to successfully tightrope walk directly over Niagara Falls.

    2012 AD

    Canada / United States
