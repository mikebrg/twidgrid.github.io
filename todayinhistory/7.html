July

Named after Julius Caesar, the Roman general and statesman. July was originally called Quintilis in the Roman calendar, meaning "fifth month," but was later renamed in honor of Caesar following his assassination in 44 B.C.

    Roman Empire

    Roman Empire

  • Thomas Savery patents the first steam engine.

    1698 AD

    London, England

  • The AbioCor self-contained artificial heart is first implanted.

    2001 AD

    United States

    324 AD

    Adrianople (modern Edirne, Turkey)

    1886 AD

    Germany

  • World speed record for a steam locomotive is set in England, by the Mallard, which reaches a speed of 125.88 miles per hour (202.58 km/h).

    1938 AD

    London, England

    362 BC

    Mantineia, Arcadia

    1862 AD

    England

    1997 AD

    Mars

    328 AD

    1841 AD

    England

  • Jeff Bezos founds Amazon.

    1994 AD

    Bellevue, Washington, United States

    371 BC

    1885 AD

    France

    2003 AD

    Yevpatoria, Crimea, Ukraine
