WebSearch

Search the Web and Discover the World.

Top Web Search Engines

  • Google

    The heavyweight champ. Massive index, scary-good relevance, owns the planet.

  • Bing

    Microsoft’s engine; powers a lot of “secondary” searches and does solid image/video search.

  • Yahoo Search

    Still alive! Mostly powered by Bing under the hood.

  • DuckDuckGo

    Privacy-first, no tracking, no filter bubble stalking you around the web.

  • Baidu

    Dominant in China; basically “Google, but Chinese-market focused.”

  • Yandex

    Big in Russia and parts of Eastern Europe; strong with local language search.

  • Brave Search

    Independent index, privacy-focused, growing fast.

  • Naver

    South Korea’s go-to search engine; very ecosystem-driven.

  • Ecosia

    Uses Bing results, but plants trees with ad revenue 🌱

  • Startpage

    Google results without Google tracking you like a lost puppy.

  • Ask.com

    Originally Ask Jeeves. Now more Q&A/content-driven than pure search.

  • AOL Search

    Still operates search (powered largely by Bing).

  • Wolfram Alpha

    Not a traditional crawler — it computes answers. Amazing for math, finance, data queries.

  • Swisscows

    Privacy-focused, family-friendly filtering built in.

  • Mojeek

    Runs its own crawler and index — rare in today’s Bing/Google-powered world.

  • Qwant

    European privacy-oriented engine.

  • SearXNG

    Open-source metasearch engine — can be self-hosted (very developer-friendly 👀).

  • Gibiru

    Markets itself as “uncensored” search.

  • You.com

    AI-powered hybrid search + chatbot experience.

  • Perplexity AI

    Conversational AI search with cited answers.

  • Dogpile

    Classic metasearch engine that pulls results from Google, Bing, and Yahoo simultaneously.

  • 100 Search Engines

    Clusters search results by topic, great for research and discovery.

  • Oscobo

    UK-based, privacy-friendly engine with encrypted searches and no tracking.
