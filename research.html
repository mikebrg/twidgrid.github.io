Research

These platforms are essential for academic research and provide comprehensive tools for accessing and analyzing scholarly materials.

Google Scholar

A widely used platform for accessing scholarly articles, books, patents, and court opinions. It provides comprehensive search capabilities and citation tracking, supporting a broad range of academic fields.

PubMed

Focused on biomedical and life sciences research, PubMed is an essential tool for medical professionals and researchers. It offers free access to MEDLINE and links to full-text resources​.

BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search Engine)

Specializes in aggregating research from institutional repositories worldwide, with over 136 million documents, many of which are open access.

Semantic Scholar

An AI-powered research tool designed to enhance the discovery of relevant studies, identify connections, and prioritize significant findings in scientific literature.

CORE

A repository offering open access to millions of academic papers, facilitating free and easy discovery of scholarly content​.

ScienceDirect

A repository of scientific and technical research, providing access to journals, books, and articles in fields like engineering, health, and life sciences.

Web of Science

A robust citation indexing service that tracks journal impact factors and connects researchers to the most influential studies in various disciplines.

Scopus

Similar to Web of Science, Scopus offers comprehensive bibliometric tools and covers a vast array of academic journals, conferences, and patents.

Science.gov

Consolidates search results from over 15 U.S. federal agencies, offering a treasure trove of scientific publications and reports.

ERIC (Education Resources Information Center)

Aimed at educators and researchers, ERIC offers access to education-related literature, including articles, reports, and conference proceedings.

RefSeek

Aimed at students and early-career researchers, RefSeek indexes over one billion academic documents, making it a user-friendly search tool for scholarly information.

JSTOR

A digital library offering access to thousands of academic journals, books, and primary sources across various disciplines, particularly humanities and social sciences​.
