Peugeot Rifter

The Peugeot Rifter is a versatile and practical van-based people carrier that has transitioned to an all-electric vehicle (EV) in many markets (known as the Peugeot e-Rifter), focusing on family-friendly practicality and generous interior space.

Key Features

The Rifter is built on Peugeot's modular EMP2 platform and is available in two lengths, "Standard" and "Long", offering flexible seating configurations and impressive cargo capacity.

Design and Modularity: It features a robust, SUV-inspired exterior design with increased ground clearance. The interior is highly adaptable, with options for 5 or 7 seats and individual folding rear seats. The front passenger seat can also be folded flat to accommodate long items up to 3 meters in the Long version.

Practicality: A key highlight is its vast storage space, including numerous cubbies and an impressive boot capacity ranging from 775 litres to a maximum of 4,000 litres with the rear seats folded down in the Long version. It also features convenient sliding side doors and an optional opening rear tailgate window.

Technology: The Rifter incorporates the PEUGEOT i-Cockpit, which includes a compact steering wheel, a head-up display, and an 8-inch or 10-inch touchscreen infotainment system with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto compatibility.

Safety: It performed well in Euro NCAP safety tests, with good protection against whiplash and effective autonomous emergency braking (AEB) systems.

Engine Options

While earlier models offered a range of petrol and diesel engines, in many regions, the Rifter is now solely available as the all-electric e-Rifter.

Peugeot e-Rifter (Electric): This version is equipped with a 100 kW (136 hp) electric motor and a 50 kWh battery, offering a WLTP range of approximately 280 km (around 170 miles). It supports fast DC charging up to 100 kW, allowing a 10 to 80% charge in about 26 minutes.

Combustion Engines (Older/Specific Markets): For those looking at used models or in specific markets, the Rifter was available with 1.2-litre petrol (110 hp) and 1.5-litre diesel engines (75 hp, 100 hp, and 130 hp). Reviewers often recommend the more powerful diesel units for the best blend of pulling power and fuel economy.

Availability

The Peugeot Rifter is a popular choice in European and other markets, and its all-electric variant is widely available across these regions. It shares its platform and design with the Citroën Berlingo and Vauxhall Combo Life/Opel Combo Life.
