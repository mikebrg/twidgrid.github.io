  • PGO Speedster II

    2002

    The PGO Speedster II is a neo-retro, two-seater roadster produced by French independent car manufacturer PGO Automobiles. It features a classic, timeless design inspired by the Porsche 356, but is built with modern components.

    Key Features and History

    Design: The car has a distinctive retro-chic appearance with a composite body mounted on a rigid, light tubular chassis.

    Production: Each Speedster II is largely handcrafted and highly customizable, leading to small production runs.

    Powertrain: The vehicle uses engines and other technical components from major manufacturers for reliability and ease of maintenance.

    Early models (from the early 2000s) were fitted with a 2.0-liter, 140 hp Peugeot engine.

    From 2012 onwards, PGO signed an official deal with BMW to use a more powerful 1.6-liter turbocharged engine producing 184 hp.

    Driving Experience: It is known for a raw, purist driving experience, with features like mid-engine placement and the absence of power steering, offering a "go-kart feeling" on open roads.

    Performance (with 2.0L engine):

    Maximum speed: 215 km/h (134 mph).

    Acceleration (0-100 km/h): Less than 7 seconds.

    Kerb weight: Approximately 980 kg (2,160 lbs).
