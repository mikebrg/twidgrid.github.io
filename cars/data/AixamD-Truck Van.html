Aixam D-Truck Van

The Aixam D-Truck Van has been in production since 2012, with various updates and engine configurations introduced over the years. The model lineup includes both diesel and electric versions, catering to different market needs. The D-Truck Van is part of Aixam's broader strategy to provide compact, license-free utility vehicles for professional use.

Initially launched in 2012, the D-Truck Van featured a 400cc diesel engine producing 5.4 horsepower. By 2015, Aixam introduced a more powerful 599cc engine delivering 11 kW (approximately 15 horsepower), expanding the vehicle's capabilities. In 2018, the D-Truck Van lineup was further diversified with the introduction of the 479cc diesel engine variant, producing 6 kW (8 horsepower), catering to markets with different regulatory requirements. In recent years, Aixam has also introduced electric versions of the D-Truck Van, aligning with the industry's shift towards sustainable mobility solutions. These electric models offer the same compact size and utility features as their diesel counterparts but with zero emissions, making them suitable for urban environments with strict emissions regulations.

Throughout its production, the D-Truck Van has maintained its reputation for being a reliable and versatile compact utility vehicle. Its small size and payload capacity make it ideal for businesses operating in urban areas or confined spaces. The vehicle's design emphasizes practicality, with features tailored to meet the needs of professionals seeking a compact yet capable transport solution. Whether powered by diesel or electricity, the D-Truck Van continues to be a popular choice for businesses requiring a license-free vehicle that doesn't compromise on performance or utility.
