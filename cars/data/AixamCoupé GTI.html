Aixam Coupé GTI

The Aixam Coupé GTI was produced from 2014 to 2020.

The Aixam Coupé GTI, a compact and sporty microcar, entered production in 2014. It was designed to cater to young drivers, particularly those who could drive with an AM licence in Europe. The model featured a 400cc engine, delivering 5.4 horsepower, and weighed approximately 350 kg. Its design included sporty elements such as a glossy black honeycomb grille, LED indicator lights on wing mirrors, scrolling front and rear indicators, and 16" black diamond-finish alloy wheel rims .

In 2016, Aixam introduced the e-Coupé GTI, an electric version of the Coupé GTI. This model maintained the sporty design elements of its predecessor but offered an eco-friendly alternative with electric propulsion. The e-Coupé GTI was available in various metallic colors, including silver, titanium, blue, black, red, pearl white, and yellow.

Production of the Aixam Coupé GTI continued until 2020, after which it was succeeded by newer models in Aixam's lineup. Despite its discontinuation, the Coupé GTI remains a notable example of Aixam's commitment to providing stylish and accessible vehicles for young drivers in Europe.
