Peugeot 208

The Peugeot 208 is a stylish and popular subcompact (B-segment) hatchback, highly regarded for its distinctive design, upmarket interior, and diverse range of available powertrains, including fully electric, mild-hybrid, and traditional combustion engines.

Key Features and Design

The second-generation Peugeot 208, launched in 2019 and updated in 2024, is built on the Common Modular Platform (CMP), which accommodates all its powertrain options without compromising interior space.

Design: It features a bold, eye-catching exterior with a large, body-coloured grille, the brand's new emblem, and signature three-claw LED daytime running lights.

Interior: The cabin showcases the innovative PEUGEOT i-Cockpit, which includes a small, flattened steering wheel designed to be looked over to a digital instrument display, which features a 3D effect on top-tier models. The infotainment system is accessed via a 7-inch or 10-inch touchscreen, with most models supporting Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.

Practicality: It offers 311 litres of boot space, which is average for the segment and slightly better than some rivals like the Ford Fiesta.

Engine Options

The Peugeot 208 offers a comprehensive range of engine options tailored to different driver preferences.

Petrol (PureTech):

1.2L PureTech 75: A 75 hp three-cylinder engine with a 5-speed manual gearbox, the most accessible option.

1.2L PureTech 100: A 100 hp turbocharged engine available with a 6-speed manual or 8-speed automatic transmission.

1.2L PureTech 130: A 130 hp turbocharged engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

Hybrid (48V):

New for the 2024 model, a 48V mild-hybrid system is available with 100 hp or 136 hp outputs, combined with a 6-speed e-DCS6 dual-clutch automatic transmission. This system improves fuel economy and can run on electric power alone for short distances around town.

Electric (e-208):

Motor: A powerful 115 kW (156 hp) electric motor for instant acceleration.

Range: The newer version boasts a WLTP range of up to 400 km (approx. 248 miles).

Charging: It supports up to 100 kW DC fast charging, allowing a 10-80% charge in approximately 30 minutes.

Awards and Reviews

The second-generation Peugeot 208 was named the "Car of the Year 2020" in Europe, a testament to its significant improvements over its predecessor in design, quality, and powertrain diversity. Reviewers praise its ride comfort and build quality, often noting its premium feel compared to other superminis.
