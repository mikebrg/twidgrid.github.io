  • Chatenet CH40

    2018

    A microcar that’s small in size but big on personality. Manufactured by Automobiles Chatenet, a French company established in 1984, the CH40 was introduced in 2018 as a modern, stylish alternative in the world of "voitures sans permis" (light quadricycles)

    Specifications

    Engine: Lombardini Kohler 0.5L diesel engine

    Power: 6 kW (approximately 8 hp)

    Top Speed: 45 km/h (28 mph)

    Fuel Consumption: 3L per 100 km

    Fuel Tank Capacity: 14–15 liters

    Range: Up to 500 km on a full tank

    Transmission: Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

    Dimensions: Length: 2,977 mm; Width: 1,492 mm; Height: 1,457 mm

    Seating: 2 seats

    Drive Type: Front-wheel drive

    Brakes: Front and rear disc brakes with automatic wear compensation

    Suspension: Independent front suspension with MacPherson struts; independent rear suspension with trailing arms

    Steering: Mechanical rack-and-pinion with a turning radius of 4 meters

    Wheels: 14-inch aluminum wheels

    Tires: 155/65 R14

    Variants

    The CH40 lineup includes several variants to cater to different preferences:

    CH40 Junior: A more compact version, ideal for urban environments.

    CH40 Spring: A sportier model with enhanced features.

    CH40 Barooder: A crossover-style variant for those seeking a rugged look.

    Production Timeline

    The CH40 has been in production since 2018 and remains part of Chatenet's current lineup.

    Given the brand's commitment to innovation, it's likely that the CH40 will continue to evolve with updates in design and technology.

    Final Thoughts

    The Chatenet CH40 is a charming and practical choice for those seeking a compact vehicle that doesn't require a full driving license in many European countries. Its blend of efficiency, style, and versatility makes it a standout in the microcar segment.
