  • Mexico made a winning start to the 2026 FIFA World Cup

    2026, June 12

    Mexico

    Co-hosts Mexico made a winning start to the 2026 FIFA World Cup, defeating South Africa 2–0 in the opening Group A match at the iconic Estadio Azteca. scored the tournament's first goal, and added a second-half header to secure the victory.

    The match was overshadowed by a flurry of disciplinary incidents. South Africa had two players sent off— and —while Mexico's was also shown a red card late in the game. It became the first World Cup opening match in history to feature three red cards.

    The result gives Mexico an early advantage in Group A, which also includes South Korea and Czech Republic. Mexico's next match is against South Korea on June 18, while South Africa face the Czech Republic.

    Among fans online, many praised Mexico's control of the match but debated several of the red-card decisions, particularly the dismissals of Zwane and Montes. Some also felt South Africa struggled to compete, while others criticized the referee's strict approach.

    The victory was historic for Mexico: it was their first-ever win in a World Cup opening match after seven previous attempts.
