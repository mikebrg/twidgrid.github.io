  • Peysoh (born Timothy Obotuke) is a Mexican-American rapper from Maywood, California, known for his authentic West Coast storytelling and lyrics centered on his life in the streets. He rose to prominence with his 2020 viral single "6 Block," which he released at age 16.

    Mainstream Breakthrough: In late 2024, Peysoh gained significant attention for his guest appearance on Kendrick Lamar's track "GNX" from the album of the same name.

    Style: His music is categorized as contemporary West Coast rap, often compared to the styles of the late MoneySign Suede, who was a close friend and frequent collaborator.

    Early Life: Born on December 23, 2003, he grew up in Maywood, CA. He turned to music as an outlet following the death of his father to gun violence when Peysoh was six years old.

    Heritage: He is of Mexican descent; his mother is from Jalisco and his father is from Sinaloa.

    As of early 2026, Peysoh has resumed active performing and public appearances after a brief legal stint in late 2025.

    Performances: He has performed recent shows in regions like Tri-Cities, Washington, and has upcoming concerts scheduled, including a show in Adelanto, CA on May 2, 2026.

    Public Appearances: He was recently seen walking out boxer Arnold Barboza Jr. for a match in March 2026.
