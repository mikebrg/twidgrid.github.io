Tech & Computing Innovators

Some legendary, some modern disruptors, and a few rising stars you’ll definitely want to keep your digital eye on.

Search

  • Alan Turing

    Father of modern computing. If code had a godfather, it’d be him.

    1912-1954

    Known for Cryptanalysis of the Enigma, Turing's proof, Turing machine, Turing test, unorganised machine, Turing pattern, Turing reduction, The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis, Turing paradox

    Fields: Logic, mathematics, cryptanalysis, computer science, mathematical and theoretical biology

    Awards: Smith's Prize (1936)

  • Ada Lovelace

    The first computer programmer… in the 1800s. Time traveler? Maybe.

    1815-1852

    Known for Mathematics, computing

    Fields: Mathematics

  • Grace Hopper

    Created the first compiler. Also coined “debugging” (literally pulled a moth from a computer).

    1906-1992

    Known for FLOW-MATIC, COBOL

    Fields: Computer science, Mathematics

    Awards: Defense Distinguished Service Medal, Legion of Merit, Meritorious Service Medal, American Campaign Medal, World War II Victory Medal, National Defense Service Medal, Armed Forces Reserve Medal with two Hourglass Devices, Naval Reserve Medal, Presidential Medal of Freedom (posthumous)

  • John von Neumann

    Architect of the modern computer design. Every CPU today says, “thanks, John.”

    1903-1957

    Known for Mathematical formulation of quantum mechanics, Game theory, Spectral theory, Ergodic theory, von Neumann algebras

    Fields: Logic, mathematics, mathematical physics, theoretical physics, statistics, economics, computer science, theoretical biology, chemistry, computing

    Awards: Bôcher Memorial Prize (1938), Navy Distinguished Civilian Service Award (1946), Medal for Merit (1946), Medal of Freedom (1956), Enrico Fermi Award (1956), Carl-Gustaf Rossby Research Medal (1957)

  • Dennis Ritchie

    Co-creator of Unix and C. Basically invented the software backbone of the planet.

    1941-2011

    Known for ALTRAN, B, BCPL, C, Multics, Unix

    Fields: Computer science

    Awards: IEEE Emanuel R. Piore Award (1982), Turing Award (1983), National Medal of Technology (1998), IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal (1990), Computer Pioneer Award (1994), Computer History Museum Fellow (1997), Harold Pender Award (2003), Japan Prize (2011)

  • Tim Berners-Lee

    Invented the World Wide Web. The "http" guy.

    b. 8 June 1955

    Known for Invention of the World Wide Web

    Fields: Computer science

    Awards: Turing Award (2016), Queen Elizabeth Prize (2013), Foreign Associate of the National Academy of Sciences (2009), Order of Merit (2007), ACM Software System Award (1995)

  • Linus Torvalds

    Created Linux. Also gave us Git. Revered like a penguin deity in developer circles.

    b. 28 December 1969

    Known for Linux, Git

    Fields: Computer science

    Awards: 2012 Millennium Technology Prize, 2014 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award, 2018 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award

  • Steve Jobs

    Made computing beautiful, personal, and hip. The black turtleneck maestro.

    1955-2011

    Known for Pioneer of the personal computer revolution with Steve Wozniak, Co-creator of the Apple II, Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, iPad, and first Apple Stores

    Fields: Calligraphy, Philosophy & Eastern Religion, Electronics & Engineering

    Awards: 1985: National Medal of Technology, 1987: Jefferson Award for Public Service, 1989: Entrepreneur of the Decade by Inc., 1991: Howard Vollum Award, 2007: inducted into the California Hall of Fame, 2012: Grammy Trustees Award, 2012: Edison Achievement Award, 2022: Presidential Medal of Freedom

  • Bill Gates

    Brought computing to the masses and now gives malaria the middle finger.

    b. 28 October 1955

    Known for Pioneer of the personal computer revolution with Paul Allen, Co-founding of Microsoft and Gates Foundation

    Fields: Mathematics, Computer science

    Awards: Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (2005), Padma Bhushan (2015), Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016), Grand Cordon of the Order of the Rising Sun (2020), Hilal-e-Pakistan (2022)

  • Mark Zuckerberg

    Turned college procrastination into a multi-billion-dollar social empire.

    b. 14 May 1984

    Known for Meta Platforms and Facebook, Chan Zuckerberg Initiative

    Fields: Psychology, Computer science

    Awards: Axel Springer Award (2016), Horatio Alger Award (2017), Double Helix Medal (2017), Global Recognition Award (2024), MIT Technology Review Innovators Under 35

  • Geoffrey Hinton

    Godfather of Deep Learning. Neural nets? That’s his playground.

    b. 6 December 1947

    Known for Applications of backpropagation, Boltzmann machine, Deep learning, Capsule neural networks

    Fields: Machine learning, Psychology, Artificial intelligence, Cognitive science, Computer science

    Awards: Rumelhart Prize (2001), Turing Award (2018), Dickson Prize (2021), Princess of Asturias Award (2022), Nobel Prize in Physics (2024), VinFuture Prize (2024), Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025)
