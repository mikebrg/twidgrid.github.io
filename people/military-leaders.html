Military Leaders

A curated collection of the most influential military commanders who shaped empires, warfare, and history through strategy, leadership, and decisive battles.

Home Search

  • Sitting Bull

    Hunkpapa Lakota leader.

    Born Húŋkešni (Slow) or Ȟoká Psíče (Jumping Badger) c. 1831–1837 Grand River, Unorganized Territory, U.S. / Died December 15, 1890 (aged 53–57) Standing Rock Indian Reservation, Grand River, South Dakota, U.S.

    Led his people during years of resistance against United States government policies.

    Allegiance: Hunkpapa Lakota (Sioux Nation); Allied Plains tribes resisting U.S. military expansion

    Conflicts: Battle of the Little Bighorn (1876); Red Cloud’s War (1866–1868); Great Sioux War (1876–1877)

  • Hasan Pasha

    16th-century Ottoman statesman and naval commander.

    Born: c. 1517 Algiers / Died: 4 July 1572 (aged 54–55) Constantinople, Ottoman Empire

    Years active: c. 1545–1572

    Allegiance: Ottoman Algeria

    Conflicts: Expedition to Mostaganem (1547); Battle of Tlemcen (1551); Battle of Tlemcen (1557); Expedition to Mostaganem (1558); Great Siege of Malta (1565); Battle of Lepanto (1571)

  • Mochizuki Chiyome

    16th-century Japanese poet and noblewoman.

    She is rumored for allegedly creating a group of kunoichi in service of the Takeda clan.

    Era: c. 16th century (mid-1500s CE)

    Allegiance: Takeda clan, particularly Takeda Shingen; Sengoku-period provincial military authority

    Conflicts: Takeda clan campaigns during the Sengoku period, including the Battles of Kawanakajima (1553–1564).

  • Cuauhtémoc

    Last emperor (Tlatoani) of the Aztec Empire.

    Born 14 July 1496 Tenochtitlan, Aztec Empire / Died 28 February 1525 (aged 28) Acalan, New Spain (now Campeche, Mexico)

    Reign: 25 January 1521 – 28 February 1525

    Allegiance: Aztec Empire (Mexica); Defender of Tenochtitlan

    Conflicts: Siege of Tenochtitlan (1521) — final defense against Hernán Cortés

  • Ocēlōtl

    Members of the Aztec military elite.

    Elite Aztec soldiers distinguished by jaguar-themed armor and suits.

    Existed from the 14th century (c. 1325) — until 1521.

    Allegiance: Aztec Empire (Mexico), centered in Tenochtitlan

    Conflicts: The Flower Wars (c. 1450s–early 1500s); Aztec Expansion Campaigns (15th century); The Spanish–Aztec War (1519–1521)

  • William Tell

    Legendary folk hero of Switzerland.

    Era: c. late 13th – early 14th century

    Expert marksmanship with a crossbow.

    Allegiance: Swiss Confederation (legendary cantons resisting Habsburg Austria)

    Conflicts: Resistance against Habsburg overlords in the early formation of the Swiss Confederation; Legendary events leading to Swiss independence

  • Xueting Fuyu

    Abbot of the Shaolin Monastery of the Caodong lineage.

    Organizing and expanding the Shaolin monastic system.

    Era: 1203 – 1275 CE

    Allegiance: Shaolin Temple (Henan, China); Yuan dynasty religious administration; Chan (Zen) Buddhist tradition

    Conflicts: Associated with the broader military-monastic tradition of Shaolin during the Mongol/Yuan period.

  • Kandake of Nubia

    Queen mother of Kingdom of Kush.

    Era: c. 4th century BCE – 4th century CE

    Exercised independent authority and commanded armies.

    Allegiance: Kingdom of Kush (Nubia), centered at Napata and later Meroë

    Conflicts: Wars against Roman Egypt (notably under Kandake Amanirenas, c. 25–22 BCE)

  • Hermann the Cheruscan

    Germanic chieftain who famously defeated three Roman legions in one of the most decisive ambushes of antiquity.

    Born: 18/17 BC / Died: AD 21 (aged 37–38)

    Better known by his Latin name Arminius.

    Allegiance: Cherusci tribe (Germanic); Coalition of Germanic tribes opposing Roman expansion

    Conflicts: Battle of the Teutoburg Forest (9 CE)

  • Artemisia I of Caria

    Queen of the ancient Greek city-state of Halicarnassus, which is now in Bodrum, Turkey.

    Born: c. 520 BCE / Died after 480 BCE (exact death date unknown)

    Praised by Herodotus for her intelligence and tactical insight.

    Allegiance: Achaemenid Persian Empire; Loyal to King Xerxes I; Ruler of Halicarnassus (Caria)

    Conflicts: Battle of Salamis (480 BCE) — commanded five ships in Xerxes’ fleet; Broader Greco-Persian Wars (early 5th century BCE)

  • Leonidas I

    King of the Ancient Greek city-state of Sparta.

    Born c. 540 BC / Died 11 August 480 BC

    Reign: 489–480 BC

    Allegiance: Sparta

    Conflicts: Second Persian invasion of Greece: Battle of Thermopylae

  • Miltiades

    Miltiades the Younger, was a Greek Athenian statesman.

    Born 550 BC Athens / Died 489 BC (aged 60–61) Athens

    Miltiades’ strategic insight at Marathon became legendary, demonstrating the effectiveness of hoplite warfare and solidifying Athens’ military prestige in the Greco-Persian Wars.

    Allegiance: Athens (City-state of the Delian League precursor)

    Conflicts: Battle of Marathon (490 BCE) — key victory against Darius I of Persia

  • Cyrus the Great

    Cyrus II of Persia was the founder of the Achaemenid Empire.

    Born c. 600 BC Anshan, Persis, Kingdom of Anshan (now Fars, Iran) / Died 530 BC Central Asia or Persia

    One of the most influential conquerors and state-builders of the ancient world.

    Allegiance: Achaemenid Persian Empire; King of Anshan, Media, Lydia, and Babylon

    Conflicts: Conquest of the Median Empire (c. 550 BCE); Defeat of Croesus of Lydia (c. 547 BCE); Conquest of Babylon (539 BCE)
