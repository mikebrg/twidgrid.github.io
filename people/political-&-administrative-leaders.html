  • Otto von Bismarck

    German statesman and diplomat who oversaw the unification of Germany.

    Born: Otto Eduard Leopold von Bismarck-Schönhausen 1 April 1815 Schönhausen, Altmark, Prussia / Died: 30 July 1898 (aged 83) Friedrichsruh, Germany

    In office: 21 March 1871 – 18 March 1890

    Allegiance: Kingdom of Prussia; German Empire (after 1871)

    Notable Conflicts: Second Schleswig War (1864); Austro-Prussian War (1866); Franco-Prussian War (1870–1871)

  • Louis XIV

    Known as the “Sun King,” was King of France.

    Born: 5 September 1638 Château de Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Saint-Germain-en-Laye, France / Died: 1 September 1715 (aged 76) Palace of Versailles, Versailles, France

    Reign: 14 May 1643 – 1 September 1715

    Allegiance: Kingdom of France; House of Bourbon

    One of the longest-reigning monarchs in European history, symbolizing the height of absolute monarchy.

  • Mansa Musa

    Ninth Mansa of the Mali Empire.

    Born c.1280 Mali Empire / Died c.1337 (aged c.57) Mali Empire

    Reign: c.1312 – c.1337 (approx. 25 years)

    Allegiance: Musa's reign is often regarded as the zenith of Mali's power and prestige.

    Renowned for his immense wealth.

  • Aemilia Tertia

    Wife of Scipio Africanus.

    Born: c. 230 BCE / Died: c. 163 BCE

    Roman noblewoman of the 3rd–2nd century BCE.

    Allegiance: Roman Republic; Patrician gens Aemilia; Family of Scipio Africanus

    Lived during the Second Punic War (218–201 BCE); Connected to the military and political elite of Rome

  • Ashoka the Great

    Emperor of Magadha

    Born c.304 BCE / Died 232 BCE (aged 72)

    Reign: c.268 – 232 BCE

    Allegiance: Maurya Empire; Dynastic and administrative loyalty to the Mauryan state

    Notable Conflicts: Kalinga War (c. 261 BCE); Mauryan expansion campaigns.

  • Mereruka

    One of Egypt's most powerful officials.

    Born c. 2400 BC / Died c. 2345–2330 BC

    Era: c. 2345 – 2181 BCE (6th Dynasty, Old Kingdom)

    Allegiance: Pharaoh Teti of Egypt; The central administration of the Old Kingdom

    Oversaw state affairs, legal matters, and religious ceremonies.
